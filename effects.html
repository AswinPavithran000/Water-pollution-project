Effects of Water Pollution

Health Effects

Water pollution has severe effects on human health, leading to diseases such as cholera, dysentery, and hepatitis. Long-term exposure can also result in cancer and neurological disorders.

Environmental Effects

Water pollution disrupts aquatic ecosystems, harming species and depleting oxygen levels in water bodies. Eutrophication leads to algal blooms, suffocating aquatic life.

Biodiversity Loss

Pollution can destroy habitats and reduce populations of species relying on clean water, further disrupting the ecological balance.

Water Scarcity

Polluted water sources are no longer safe for consumption, contributing to water scarcity, especially in regions already struggling with limited resources.

Climate Change

Water pollution worsens the impacts of climate change by contaminating freshwater resources, making it harder to mitigate extreme weather events and rising temperatures.
